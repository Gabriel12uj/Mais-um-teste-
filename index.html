About Me

I am currently a Professor at Federal Rural University of Rio de Janeiro – UFRRJ (Department of Physics). I have obtained her degrees in Physics in 2005 (Licentiate), in 2007 (Bachelor), in 2009 (Master), and in 2013 (Doctorate) from the State University of Rio de Janeiro. I have also improved my skills through the Post doctoral research at Pontifical Catholic University of Rio de Janeiro (Laboratory of Optoelectronic – LOpEL) in 2014 and at Federal University of Rio de Janeiro (Laboratory of Instrumentation and Photonic – LIF) between 2015 and 2016.

I have always been interested in all subject related to Physics and Chemistry. I have started working as an adjunct professor at UFRRJ in 2016. I have confidence in her ability to Experimental Physics, where I have expertise in Optical Spectroscopy, Optical Fiber sensors, Nanomaterials, Metamaterials, X-ray diffraction, and Nonlinear Optics. My experiences include teaching and researching Physics, also being advisor of students and doing part of peer review process of Scientific articles.
